AI in People Operations at Storable

A concise look at how our People team is using AI to make HR work simpler, faster, and more consistent for Storriors and leaders.

Presented by Gill Godfrey, Chief People Officer, Storable.

Function: People / HR
Focus: Self‑service, quality, and leadership readiness
What you’ll see
We are using AI to:
– Help employees self‑serve on policies, leaves, and processes.
– Support managers through org design and leadership transitions.
– Shift HRBPs from Tier 1 questions to coaching and strategy.
Section 1

✨ Our AI Toolkit (People Ops View)

We use a small, opinionated stack so People Ops and business leaders see one coherent experience instead of a pile of disconnected tools.

  • Glean – Enterprise AI Platform
    Central entry point for AI across Storable (non‑engineering). Connected to 100+ data sources (Google Drive, Confluence, Slack, Salesforce, Jira, etc.) and used by People Ops for knowledge retrieval, policy and program summaries, and building no‑code agents.
  • Gemini Pro (Google Workspace AI)
    Embedded in Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Slides, and NotebookLM. We use it to draft change communications, manager talking points, workshop decks, and to analyze survey or program data directly in Workspace.
  • Lattice AI Agent (HR Help Desk)
    Answers employee questions about policies, benefits, PTO, and onboarding inside Lattice, grounded in our Employee Handbook, benefit guides, career tracks, and compliance documents.
  • Zoom AI Companion
    Provides meeting transcription and summaries for HR and leadership sessions, capturing decisions, risks, and follow‑ups so they make it into our plans and not just our calendars.
  • Claude (via Glean)
    General‑purpose assistant we reach through Glean for longer‑form writing, analysis, and web‑informed research on topics like labor trends or leave regulations.
  • LinkedIn Learning – AI Skill Paths
    Curated AI learning paths, including Gemini Pro content, to help managers, HRBPs, and ICs build practical skills at their own pace.
  • AI Policy & Guardrails
    Our AI Tool Usage Guidelines and AI Policy live on Confluence and set expectations for responsible use, data security, and which tools are approved. People Ops relies on these to keep experimentation safe and intentional.
Section 2

🤖 People Ops Agents We’ve Built

These are live or in‑flight agents that directly support our employees, managers, and HR teams. All are built on Glean Agent Builder using a discovery → scope → build → QA → deploy process.

  • Leave Management Agent
    Helps US employees understand state‑specific leave laws, how to apply, and who is involved, then notifies the employee’s manager, HRBP, and Benefits Manager automatically.
    Sources: ADP employee data, Employee Handbook, state leave laws. Impact focus: Less back‑and‑forth email and faster, more consistent leave decisions.
  • Job Description Agent
    Converts existing job descriptions into Storable’s standardized JD template and guides hiring managers to create new JDs or reuse the best existing ones.
    Sources: Google Drive JD library. Impact focus: Faster JD turnaround and clearer alignment with leveling and compensation frameworks.
  • Run of Show Agent
    Orchestrates VP‑level and above leadership transitions and senior exits by building a detailed run‑of‑show for who to inform, in what order, and on what timeline before any announcement goes live.
    Sources: Leadership transition checklists, HR playbooks, communications templates, Google Calendar and Drive planning documents. Impact focus: De‑risks leadership changes by keeping stakeholder communications, access changes, and talking points aligned and proactive.
  • Org Chart Builder Agent
    Generates up‑to‑date, navigable org charts by pulling reporting lines and team data from our HR systems and shared folders.
    Sources: ADP employee data, Google Drive org and HR documents. Impact focus: Gives leaders and HRBPs a single source of truth for team structures during planning, onboarding, and reorganizations.
  • Storrior HRBP Agent
    Answers employee and manager questions about HR processes, policies, documentation, and performance coaching, and directs people to the right resources when a human needs to step in.
    Sources: Google Drive, Confluence, Employee Handbook. Impact focus: Deflects Tier 1 “how do I…?” questions so HRBPs can spend more time on coaching, org design, and leadership support.
Section 3

📚 How We’re Building AI Competency in People Ops

We treat AI as a capability the People team owns and grows over time, not a one‑off project. That means structured training, embedded enablement, and clear next bets.

Training Programs in Motion

  • Glean Enablement Sessions
    Company‑wide series (three core sessions) on Search, Chat, and Agent Builder, plus roughly 100 additional hours of office hours and deep‑dive sessions for specific departments.
  • Gemini Pro Workshop Series (via Promevo)
    Four‑part workshop series (April–May 2026) on Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Slides, and NotebookLM. A $6K investment with 50 live seats per session; all sessions recorded to Absorb LMS and Confluence for ongoing access.
  • Virtual AI Summit – People Operations
    Internal summit focused on HR automation opportunities: ramp plans, Tier 1 HR Q&A agents, and administrative workflows like change‑of‑status letters and transition agreements.
  • AI Enablement Roles
    Dedicated AI enablement roles embedded with engineering and business teams to support adoption, surface use cases, and co‑build agents with People Ops as a key stakeholder.
  • LinkedIn Learning AI Paths
    Curated AI learning journeys with a focus on Gemini Pro and day‑to‑day AI skills for managers and ICs.

What We’re Investing in Next

  • Advanced Agent Building
    Moving from Q&A agents to multi‑step workflow agents that can take actions (for example creating Jira tickets, sending emails, or updating Salesforce) with appropriate approvals and oversight.
  • Prompt Engineering at Scale
    Helping each department write clear prompts and agent instructions so front‑line teams can safely design agents that reflect how their work actually runs.
  • AI Governance and Responsible Use
    Continuing education on data security, bias, and compliance as agents gain more autonomy, anchored in our AI usage guidelines and policy.
  • Department‑Specific Use Case Workshops
    Bringing our Glean Partner directly into team meetings to co‑design high‑impact agents that map to each function’s workflows and metrics.
  • Measuring ROI
    Defining standard ways to track time saved, ticket deflection, and quality improvements from AI agents so we can double‑down on what works and sunset what doesn’t.
